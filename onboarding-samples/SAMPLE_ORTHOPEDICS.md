## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 immediately:**
- Open fracture (bone visible through skin)
- Joint dislocation with neurovascular compromise (loss of pulse or sensation)
- Post-surgical signs of pulmonary embolism (sudden shortness of breath, chest pain)
- Deep infection with fever after joint replacement (redness, heat, discharge)
- Compartment syndrome signs (severe pain, tightness, numbness after fracture)

**Urgent same-day (call clinic):**
- New fracture suspected after fall or trauma
- Post-surgical wound opening or unusual drainage
- Sudden severe joint pain / locking of joint

**Escalation contact:** Nurse coordinator — (512) 555-0505 | OR scheduler — ext. 3

**After-hours:** "Our office is closed. For life-threatening emergencies or acute fractures, go to the nearest ER or call 911. For post-surgical concerns, call our after-hours line at (512) 555-0501. For non-urgent scheduling, call back during office hours or visit peakortho.com."

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: Do I need a referral to see an orthopedic surgeon?**
A1: Not always. PPO plans generally allow direct access. HMO plans typically require a referral from your PCP. We can help verify this when you call — have your insurance card ready.

**Q2: How long is recovery from knee replacement?**
A2: Most patients walk with assistance the same day and return to normal daily activities in 6–12 weeks. Full recovery for high-impact activities takes 3–6 months. Dr. Cole will give you a personalized timeline after your evaluation.

**Q3: Is PRP covered by insurance?**
A3: Most insurance plans classify PRP as experimental and do not cover it. It is typically $600–$800 self-pay. We offer CareCredit financing.

**Q4: Can I get a cortisone shot today for my knee pain?**
A4: Same-day injection appointments may be available. Call by 9am and we will check the injection clinic schedule for that afternoon.

**Q5: Do you treat workers' compensation injuries?**
A5: Yes. Dr. Cole is experienced in workers' comp cases. You will need your claim number, employer name, and adjuster contact information at your first visit.

**Q6: My child injured their knee playing soccer — do you see teens?**
A6: Yes, we see patients 12 and older for sports injuries. For children under 12, we recommend a pediatric orthopedic specialist.

**Q7: What should I bring to my first appointment?**
A7: All prior X-rays and MRI images (CD or digital), a list of current medications, your insurance card and photo ID, and a completed new patient form (sent to your email after booking).

**Q8: How do I know if I need surgery or just physical therapy?**
A8: Dr. Cole and Dr. Park always explore non-surgical options first. Surgery is only recommended when conservative treatment (PT, injections, rest) has not provided relief or structural damage is confirmed on imaging.

**Q9: Will I need physical therapy after surgery?**
A9: Yes — PT is an essential part of recovery. We work closely with preferred PT partners and will give you a referral at your post-op visit.

**Q10: How long does a cortisone injection take to work?**
A10: Most patients feel relief within 3–5 days. The injection itself takes less than 10 minutes. Avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours after the injection.
