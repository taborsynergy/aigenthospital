## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 immediately (child):**
- Difficulty breathing, wheezing, blue lips
- Seizure (first-time or prolonged over 5 minutes)
- High fever in newborn under 2 months (any fever = emergency)
- Severe allergic reaction after vaccine (hives, swelling, vomiting)
- Unresponsive or limp infant
- Severe head injury
- Ingestion of toxic substance → also call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

**Urgent same-day:**
- Fever over 104°F in child over 6 months
- Ear pain / possible ear infection
- Suspected pink eye (conjunctivitis)
- Rash with fever
- Possible strep throat

**Escalation contact:** Nurse on duty — press 2 on main line

**After-hours:** "Our office is closed. For life-threatening emergencies call 911.
For after-hours nurse advice, call (512) 555-0411.
For Poison Control call 1-800-222-1222.

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: My baby has a fever — when should I be worried?**
A1: Any fever in a baby under 2 months old is an emergency — go to the ER immediately. For children over 2 months, a fever above 104°F or lasting more than 3 days needs same-day evaluation. Call us and we will get you in today.

**Q2: How do I schedule my newborn's first visit?**
A2: We recommend the first newborn visit within 2–3 days of leaving the hospital. Call us before delivery to get on our schedule, or call right after discharge. We have dedicated morning newborn slots.

**Q3: Do you follow the standard vaccination schedule?**
A3: Yes, we follow the CDC-recommended immunization schedule. We do ask that all patients in our practice follow the schedule for the safety of all children, including newborns who cannot yet be vaccinated.

**Q4: My child has ADHD — can you help?**
A4: Yes. Dr. Walsh specializes in ADHD evaluation and management for children ages 5–18. The process involves parent and teacher questionnaires, behavioral rating scales, and typically 2 appointments. Book an ADHD consultation.

**Q5: What vaccinations does my child need for kindergarten?**
A5: Texas requires DTaP, Polio, MMR, Varicella, and Hepatitis B. We will review your child's records and give any missing vaccines at the 4–5 year well visit. We provide school documentation.

**Q6: My child has asthma — do you manage that?**
A6: Yes. We manage mild to moderate pediatric asthma and work with pulmonology for complex cases. We provide written asthma action plans for school.

**Q7: How do I get a sports physical for my teenager?**
A7: Sports physicals are 20–30 minutes and cost $80 self-pay (most insurance covers). Book at least 3 weeks before tryouts — they fill up fast in August and January.

**Q8: Can I talk to a nurse after hours?**
A8: Yes — call (512) 555-0411 for our after-hours nurse line available 5pm–10pm weekdays and all day weekends.

**Q9: My child was diagnosed with diabetes by another doctor. Can you manage it?**
A9: We manage type 1 and type 2 diabetes in children and will coordinate with a pediatric endocrinologist for complex cases. Please bring all prior records and device downloads (CGM, insulin pump) to the first visit.

**Q10: Do you see teenagers for mental health issues?**
A10: We do mental health screenings for depression and anxiety for patients 12 and older. For ongoing mental health treatment, we refer to a pediatric psychologist or psychiatrist. Book an adolescent wellness visit.
