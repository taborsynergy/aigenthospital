## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 or L&D immediately (OB):**
- Active labor (contractions under 5 minutes apart)
- Water breaking (SROM — spontaneous rupture of membranes)
- Vaginal bleeding in pregnancy (any trimester)
- Severe headache + visual changes + upper right abdominal pain (possible preeclampsia)
- Decreased or absent fetal movement
- Signs of ectopic pregnancy: sharp one-sided pelvic pain + vaginal bleeding in early pregnancy

**Emergency → 911 or ER (GYN):**
- Severe pelvic pain with fainting or shoulder pain (possible ectopic or ovarian torsion)
- Heavy vaginal bleeding soaking more than one pad per hour

**Urgent same-day (call clinic):**
- Fever above 101°F in a pregnant patient
- UTI symptoms in pregnancy (burning, frequency — treat urgently in pregnancy)
- Abnormal discharge or pelvic pain needing same-day evaluation

**Escalation contact:** OB nurse on duty — (512) 555-0702 (24/7 answering service)

**After-hours:** "For pregnancy emergencies such as bleeding, water breaking, or active labor, please go to St. David's Women's Center Labor & Delivery (call (512) 555-0700 for our answering service first). For non-urgent questions, leave a message and we will call back next business day."

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: How early should I schedule my first prenatal visit?**
A1: As soon as you get a positive pregnancy test. Ideally at 8–10 weeks. We will confirm your due date, check baby's heartbeat, and start your prenatal care. Call us right away — first prenatal slots fill up quickly!

**Q2: Does insurance cover my annual Pap smear?**
A2: Most insurance plans cover an annual well-woman exam (including Pap smear) at 100% with no copay. We verify your benefits before your visit. Bring your insurance card.

**Q3: Do you deliver at a hospital or birth center?**
A3: Dr. Foster delivers at St. David's Women's Center, a full-service hospital with Level III NICU. We do not deliver at birth centers or attend home births.

**Q4: Can I get an IUD at my annual exam?**
A4: Yes. IUD insertion can be done at a dedicated appointment (not same day as annual exam). We will discuss options (Mirena, Kyleena, Paragard) at your visit and schedule insertion at a separate appointment.

**Q5: Do you treat PCOS?**
A5: Yes. Dr. Foster manages PCOS with hormone therapy, lifestyle counseling, and medications like metformin or spironolactone as appropriate. Book a GYN consultation.

**Q6: What is the global OB fee and how does it work?**
A6: The global fee bundles all prenatal visits, the delivery, and your postpartum visits into one charge — typically $2,500–$3,500 self-pay. Your insurance pays most or all of this; we bill them directly. You are responsible for any copay or deductible. We review your specific coverage at your first prenatal visit.

**Q7: I'm going through menopause — can you help with symptoms?**
A7: Yes. Dr. Foster offers menopause management including hormone replacement therapy (HRT), non-hormonal options, and lifestyle counseling. Book a menopause consultation.

**Q8: Can I get STI testing without an exam?**
A8: Yes — we offer discreet STI panel testing. A brief consultation is required on the first visit. All results are confidential.

**Q9: I had a C-section before — can I try a vaginal birth this time?**
A9: Possibly. VBAC candidacy depends on your prior surgical history and current pregnancy. Dr. Foster will review your records and discuss the risks and benefits at your first prenatal visit.

**Q10: Do you see patients for postpartum depression?**
A10: Yes. We screen all postpartum patients using the Edinburgh scale. If you are struggling emotionally after delivery, please tell us — we provide support, medication management, and referrals to perinatal mental health specialists.
