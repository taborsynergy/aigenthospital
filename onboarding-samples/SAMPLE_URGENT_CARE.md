## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 immediately (DO NOT drive to urgent care):**
- Chest pain, pressure, or tightness (possible heart attack)
- Difficulty breathing or sudden shortness of breath
- Signs of stroke: facial drooping, arm weakness, slurred speech, sudden severe headache
- Severe allergic reaction: throat swelling, difficulty swallowing, loss of consciousness
- Major trauma (car accident, fall from height, head injury with loss of consciousness)
- Uncontrolled severe bleeding
- Seizure (new onset or prolonged over 5 minutes)
- Overdose or suspected poisoning → also call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222

**What urgent care CAN'T do:**
- IV fluids beyond basic hydration for minor dehydration
- Cardiac monitoring and intervention
- Psychiatric holds or emergency mental health intervention
- Major fracture reduction or orthopedic surgery

**Escalation contact:** Charge nurse — (512) 555-0909 | Office manager — ext. 0

**After-hours:** "Our clinic opens at 8am Monday–Friday and 9am Saturday, 10am Sunday. For life-threatening emergencies, call 911 or go to the nearest ER. For Poison Control, call 1-800-222-1222. For non-emergency concerns, you can also check our website for online check-in."

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: Do I need an appointment?**
A1: No! We are a walk-in clinic — come in anytime during open hours. We recommend online check-in at quickcareaustin.com to hold your spot and reduce wait time.

**Q2: How long is the wait?**
A2: Average wait is 15–30 minutes. Online check-in lets you wait from home and arrive when it's your turn. Waits may be longer during flu season (November–March).

**Q3: Can you treat a broken bone?**
A3: We can evaluate, X-ray, and splint suspected fractures. For complex fractures requiring surgery or casting, we will refer you to an orthopedic specialist and provide a splint to keep you stable in the meantime.

**Q4: Do you do COVID testing?**
A4: Yes — rapid antigen tests with results in 15 minutes. PCR testing is also available (results in 24–48 hours). Both are available for walk-ins; no appointment needed.

**Q5: Does insurance cover urgent care?**
A5: Most insurance plans cover urgent care at a copay (typically $30–$75). We verify your benefits in real time. If your plan has an HSA/FSA, you can use that card too.

**Q6: Can I get a work excuse note?**
A6: Yes. If you are seen by our provider, we can provide a return-to-work / school note upon discharge.

**Q7: Do you do DOT physicals?**
A7: Yes. Dr. Reyes is an NRCME-certified medical examiner. DOT physicals are $95 and take about 45 minutes. Book an occupational medicine appointment.

**Q8: My child has a fever of 103°F — should I come in?**
A8: Yes, bring your child in. We see patients of all ages. Fever above 103°F in a child over 2 months warrants same-day evaluation. If your infant is under 2 months with any fever, go to the ER — do not come to urgent care.

**Q9: Can you give IV fluids for dehydration?**
A9: We can provide basic IV hydration for mild-to-moderate dehydration (e.g., from stomach bug, heat exhaustion). Severe dehydration requires an ER with full monitoring capability.

**Q10: Do you prescribe antibiotics for a UTI?**
A10: Yes. If your urinalysis confirms a UTI, we will prescribe appropriate antibiotics. Most patients leave with a prescription and feel better within 24–48 hours.
