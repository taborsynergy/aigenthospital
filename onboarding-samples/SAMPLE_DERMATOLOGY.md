## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 immediately:**
- Signs of anaphylaxis after cosmetic injection (throat swelling, difficulty breathing)
- Rapidly spreading red/hot rash with fever (possible cellulitis/sepsis)
- Severe facial swelling after filler (vascular occlusion — MEDICAL EMERGENCY)
- Uncontrolled bleeding after procedure

**Urgent same-day (call clinic):**
- Suspected melanoma (new dark/irregular mole)
- Rapidly changing or bleeding lesion
- Severe allergic rash covering large body area

**Escalation contact:** Clinical coordinator — (512) 555-0305

**After-hours:** "For urgent skin emergencies such as severe allergic reactions or rapidly spreading infections, please go to the nearest ER or call 911. For non-urgent concerns, leave a message and we will call back next business day."

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: Do you treat acne?**
A1: Yes! We treat all types of acne from mild blackheads to severe cystic acne. Dr. Okonkwo specializes in acne for all skin tones. Book a consultation today.

**Q2: I found a suspicious mole — how soon can I be seen?**
A2: We take this seriously. We prioritize these appointments — call us and we will get you in within 1–3 business days.

**Q3: Does insurance cover Botox or fillers?**
A3: Cosmetic procedures are self-pay only. Insurance covers medical dermatology (acne, eczema, skin cancer screening, biopsies).

**Q4: How long does Botox last?**
A4: Results typically last 3–4 months. We recommend follow-up treatments 3 times per year to maintain results.

**Q5: What should I do before a cosmetic appointment?**
A5: Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen, fish oil) for 7 days before fillers. No retinoids for 3 days before chemical peel. We will send full prep instructions upon booking.

**Q6: Do you treat skin of color?**
A6: Yes. Dr. Okonkwo has specialized training in dermatology for melanin-rich skin tones and is experienced with hyperpigmentation, keloids, and treatments safe for darker skin.

**Q7: How long does a full body skin check take?**
A7: About 20–30 minutes. We recommend one annually for everyone and every 6 months if you have a history of skin cancer.

**Q8: Can I get a biopsy result by phone?**
A8: Yes. Dr. Okonkwo's team calls with all biopsy results within 5–7 business days. You can also view results in your patient portal.

**Q9: What is Mohs surgery?**
A9: Mohs is a highly precise skin cancer removal surgery done layer by layer with same-day results. It has the highest cure rate for basal and squamous cell carcinoma. Book a consultation with Dr. Tran.

**Q10: Is laser hair removal painful?**
A10: Most patients describe it as a mild snapping sensation. We offer topical numbing cream for sensitive areas. Results are permanent after 6–8 sessions.
