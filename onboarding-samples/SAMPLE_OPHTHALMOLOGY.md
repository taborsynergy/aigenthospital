## SECTION 8 — ESCALATION & EMERGENCY

**Emergency → 911 or ER immediately:**
- Sudden complete vision loss in one or both eyes
- Chemical splash in eye (also: flush eye with water for 20 minutes immediately, then go to ER)
- Penetrating eye injury (foreign object embedded in eye — do NOT rub)
- Signs of acute angle-closure glaucoma: severe eye pain, headache, nausea, halos around lights, sudden vision blur

**Urgent same-day (call clinic):**
- Sudden increase in floaters + flashing lights (possible retinal detachment)
- Sudden red eye with pain and light sensitivity
- Eye trauma from blunt force (punch, sports injury, fall)
- Sudden partial vision loss or "curtain" across vision

**Escalation contact:** Ophthalmic technician on duty — (512) 555-0606 | After-hours emergency pager: (512) 555-0607

## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: What is the difference between an ophthalmologist and an optometrist?**
A1: An ophthalmologist (Dr. Nguyen) is a medical doctor who performs surgery, treats eye diseases, and prescribes medications. An optometrist (Dr. Kim) provides vision exams, prescriptions for glasses and contacts, and manages routine eye health. We have both — you'll be directed to the right provider based on your needs.

**Q2: Does Medicare cover cataract surgery?**
A2: Yes, Medicare Part B covers cataract surgery with a standard lens. If you choose a premium lens (toric or multifocal), you pay the upgrade cost out of pocket ($1,500–$2,500 per eye). We will walk you through all options at your consultation.

**Q3: I see floaters and flashing lights — is that serious?**
A3: New-onset floaters with flashing lights can be a sign of retinal detachment, which is a medical emergency. Call us immediately for a same-day appointment. If you experience a sudden "curtain" or shadow in your vision, go to the ER right away.

**Q4: How often should I have my eyes checked?**
A4: Adults with no issues: every 1–2 years. Diabetics and glaucoma patients: every 6–12 months as directed. Children: annually. Over age 65: annually.

**Q5: Is LASIK available here?**
A5: We perform LASIK co-management — we do the evaluation, pre-op testing, and all post-op care. Surgery is performed at a LASIK center we partner with. Book a LASIK candidacy evaluation with Dr. Kim.

**Q6: My child failed a school vision screening — what do I do?**
A6: Book a pediatric vision exam with Dr. Kim. School screenings only detect basic acuity problems; we do a full evaluation including eye alignment, focusing, and health of the eye.

**Q7: What are my options for cataract surgery lenses?**
A7: Standard lenses (covered by insurance) correct distance vision; you'll need reading glasses after. Premium options include toric lenses for astigmatism correction and multifocal lenses for distance + reading. Dr. Nguyen will recommend the best option at your consultation.

**Q8: How long does a dilated eye exam take?**
A8: About 60–90 minutes including dilation time. Your vision will be blurry for 2–4 hours after. Please bring sunglasses and arrange a driver if possible.

**Q9: Do you offer dry eye treatment?**
A9: Yes. We offer artificial tears recommendations, prescription eye drops (Restasis, Xiidra), punctal plugs, and in-office meibomian gland treatments. Book a dry eye evaluation.

**Q10: Can I get my glasses prescription filled here?**
A10: We can provide your prescription for glasses after your exam. We have a small selection of frames in the office; you're also welcome to take your prescription to any optical shop.
