## SECTION 9 — TOP FAQs

**Q1: Do you accept Medicare?**
A1: Yes, we are a Medicare-participating provider. Please bring your Medicare card to your visit.

**Q2: Can I get a same-day appointment for the flu?**
A2: Yes! We hold sick visit slots daily. Call before 9am for the best chance of a same-day slot.

**Q3: How do I get a prescription refill?**
A3: Call us or send a request through the patient portal. Allow 48 hours. Controlled substances require an in-person visit.

**Q4: Do you do annual physicals?**
A4: Yes — we highly recommend an annual physical. Most insurance plans cover it 100%. Book one today!

**Q5: Can I get a referral to a specialist?**
A5: Yes. Dr. Rivera or Dr. Patel will review your case and provide a referral. Some insurance plans require pre-authorization — we handle that paperwork for you.

**Q6: Do you treat children?**
A6: We see patients 5 years and older. For children under 5, we recommend a pediatrician.

**Q7: How long will it take to get my lab results?**
A7: Routine labs take 2–3 business days. We will call you with results or post them to your portal.

**Q8: Can I do a telehealth visit?**
A8: Yes! Telehealth is available for follow-up visits, prescription reviews, and minor illness consultations. Book a telehealth slot Monday, Wednesday, or Friday 4–5pm.

**Q9: Do you offer COVID or flu vaccinations?**
A9: Yes — flu shots are available September through March. COVID boosters available year-round. No appointment needed for vaccinations.

**Q10: What should I bring to my first visit?**
A10: Photo ID, insurance card, list of current medications, and any recent lab results or specialist reports.
